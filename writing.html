Welcome to Writing 1002!

Begin by watching this introductory video: Writing for University- The Basics.

Writing 1002 is an introduction to academic writing for first-year ESL students in all disciplines. Topics range from grammar, sentence structure, and paragraphing to the principles of scholarly argument and research. This half course is restricted to students who use English as a Second Language, and should not be taken by students who have already taken the former Writing 0002F/G, Writing 1020F/G, 1021F/G, 1022F/G, 2101F/G.

Writing 1002 can help you think more critically and write more effectively and professionally in English, when you use it for academic purposes. It can improve your performance in all your university courses. To succeed in this 12-week course, log in to this OWL course site at least once a week, but better still, twice a week, for 5 to 6 hours, and complete the reading, lessons, and writing. 

This course is "asynchronous," which means that we won't meet as a class at a particular time. Instead, you'll follow the week-by-week course schedule, making sure you meet deadlines.

Course Instructor: Professor Patrick Morley

 

My name is Patrick Morley, and I am your professor. I'd like to say ni hao, as-salaam aleikum, annyeong haseyo, namaste, and hello to you all. I have taught in the King's Writing Program for over 10 years, and my expertise is in teaching students whose first language is not English. Its my goal to create a welcoming space for all of my students where they feel confident in expressing their ideas. I also am a journalist, and a Writing Consultant at The Write Place, the writing help centre at King's. 

If you have the right browser requirements (the latest version of accepted web browsers such as Firefox or Google Chrome), and if you are ready to get started, open the Getting Started tab on the left-hand menu. Once you've figured out what's where, click on the Syllabus tab and read through the course syllabus carefully.  Also read through the Course Supports information at the bottom of this page for more information on technical support and resources that can help you navigate life as an online learning and the academic and wellness supports available to you as a student. All the best!
